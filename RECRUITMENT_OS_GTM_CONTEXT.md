## Sales Process

### Overview

```
[Awareness] → [Connection] → [Conversation] → [Discovery] → [Proposal] → [Negotiation] → [Close]
```

### Stage 1: Connection (LinkedIn)

**Goal:** Get accepted and start conversation

**Connection Request Templates:**

*Template A (Mutual Connection):*
> "Hi [Name], I saw we're both connected to [Mutual]. I work with recruitment agencies on AI automation—noticed you're scaling [Company]. Would love to connect."

*Template B (Content Engagement):*
> "Hi [Name], loved your post about [topic]. I've been implementing AI systems for recruitment agencies—would be great to connect and exchange ideas."

*Template C (Direct Value):*
> "Hi [Name], I helped a recruitment agency 2x their revenue in 90 days using AI systems. We're now working with UK agencies. Worth connecting?"

### Stage 2: Conversation

**Goal:** Qualify interest, move to discovery call

**Message 1 (After connection accepted):**
> "Thanks for connecting, [Name]! Quick question—how is [Company] currently handling candidate sourcing? Curious if you're using any automation or still mostly manual."

**Message 2 (If they respond with pain):**
> "That's exactly what I hear from most agencies. We recently helped a German agency go from 200 manual searches/day to 40,000 automated. Cut their sourcing time by 80%. Would you be open to a 15-min call to see if something similar could work for [Company]?"

**Message 3 (If no response to M1):**
> "No worries if sourcing isn't top of mind right now. I'm sharing a case study on how we helped a recruitment agency 2x revenue in 90 days—happy to send it over if useful?"

### Stage 3: Discovery Call

**Duration:** 30 minutes

**Structure:**

1. **Rapport (2 min)**
   - Thank them for time
   - Confirm agenda

2. **Understand Their World (10 min)**
   - "Tell me about [Company]—what's your focus/specialization?"
   - "How big is your team currently?"
   - "What does your typical recruiting workflow look like?"
   - "Where do you source most of your jobs from?"
   - "What's your current tech stack?"

3. **Identify Pain (10 min)**
   - "What's the biggest bottleneck to growing revenue right now?"
   - "How much time does your team spend on sourcing vs. actually recruiting?"
   - "When you find a good job, how often are you competing against multiple agencies?"
   - "If you could wave a magic wand, what would you automate first?"

4. **Introduce Solution (5 min)**
   - Share relevant parts of case study
   - Connect their pain to specific system(s)
   - "Based on what you've shared, [System X] and [System Y] could address [their specific pain]"

5. **Next Steps (3 min)**
   - "Would it be helpful if I put together a custom proposal showing exactly how we'd implement this for [Company]?"
   - Book follow-up call
   - Set expectations on timeline

**Qualification Criteria (BANT+):**

| Criteria | Question | Green Flag | Red Flag |
|----------|----------|------------|----------|
| Budget | "What's your typical investment in growth initiatives?" | "$5K+/month range" | "We don't have budget for this" |
| Authority | "Who else would be involved in this decision?" | "I make these decisions" / "Just my partner" | "I'd need to go through procurement" |
| Need | "How urgent is solving this?" | "This quarter" / "ASAP" | "Maybe next year" |
| Timeline | "When would you want to start?" | "Within 30 days" | "Not sure, just exploring" |
| Fit | (Your assessment) | 10-100 recruiters, modern stack | <5 recruiters, no tech adoption |

### Stage 4: Proposal

**Proposal Structure:**

1. **Executive Summary**
   - Their situation (pain points discussed)
   - Our recommendation
   - Expected outcomes

2. **Proposed Solution**
   - Which systems we'll implement
   - How they integrate with their stack
   - Implementation timeline

3. **Case Study Reference**
   - German agency results
   - Similar before/after metrics

4. **Investment**
   - Pricing (retainer or project)
   - What's included
   - Payment terms

5. **Next Steps**
   - How to proceed
   - Start date

**Proposal Delivery:**
- Send via email with Loom video walkthrough
- Keep PDF under 5 pages
- Follow up within 48 hours

### Stage 5: Negotiation & Close

**Common Negotiation Points:**

| Ask | Response |
|-----|----------|
| "Can you do it cheaper?" | "I understand budget is a consideration. Our pricing reflects the 25-person team and proven results. However, we could start with [one system] at $2K to prove value, then expand." |
| "Can we do a pilot?" | "Absolutely. We can do a 30-day pilot with [System X] for $2,500. If you see results, we move to full implementation." |
| "We need to think about it" | "Of course. What specific concerns should I address? I want to make sure you have everything you need to make a confident decision." |
| "Can you guarantee results?" | "We can't guarantee specific numbers because every agency is different. What we guarantee is full implementation, training, and 90 days of optimization. Our German client saw 2x revenue—your results will depend on your market and execution." |

**Closing Techniques:**

1. **Assumptive Close:** "Great, let's get started. I'll send the agreement today—can you have it signed by Friday so we can kick off Monday?"

2. **Urgency (Honest):** "We can only onboard 2 new clients per month with our implementation team. If you want to start in February, we'd need to confirm this week."

3. **Pilot Offer:** "I can tell you're interested but want to reduce risk. Let's do this: 30-day pilot, one system, $2,500. If it works, we expand. If not, no hard feelings."

## Objection Handling

### Price Objections

**"$7K/month is too expensive"**

> "I hear you. Let me ask—how many placements does your team make per month currently? [They answer]. And what's your average fee? [They answer]. So one additional placement would cover the investment. Based on what we did with the German agency, we'd expect significantly more than one additional placement. But if budget is tight, we could start with a single system at $2K to prove value first."

**"We don't have budget right now"**

> "Understood. When does your budget cycle reset? [Listen]. In the meantime, I can send you some resources on what other agencies are doing with AI—that way you're ready when budget opens up. Also, some clients have justified this as a revenue investment rather than an expense line item, since it directly drives placement growth."

### Trust/Risk Objections

**"How do we know this will work for us?"**

> "Valid concern. A few things: First, the German agency we worked with was skeptical too—similar size, similar challenges. The results speak for themselves. Second, we don't disappear after setup. We're with you for ongoing optimization. Third, we can start with a smaller pilot if you want to prove it out before full commitment."

**"We've tried automation tools before and they didn't work"**

> "What tools did you try, and what happened? [Listen]. That's actually really common with self-serve tools—they require a lot of internal effort to implement and maintain. That's exactly why we do done-for-you. We implement, we train, we optimize. You don't need to become AI experts. You just need to use the systems we build."

**"I need to see more case studies"**

> "Totally fair. Right now we have one deep case study—the German agency. We're newer to the US/UK market, which is why we're being selective about early clients. The upside for you: you get more attention and customization than you would from a bigger vendor. The downside: you're taking a bit more risk. To offset that, we can do a smaller pilot to prove results."

### Timing Objections

**"We're too busy right now"**

> "I get it—recruitment is demanding. Here's the thing: the implementation is on us. Your team's involvement is maybe 2-3 hours per week during setup, mostly for training. After that, the systems save them 3-4 hours per day. The best time to build systems is when you're busy—that's when the ROI is highest."

**"Let's revisit in Q3"**

> "Happy to follow up then. Quick question: what would need to happen between now and Q3 for this to become a priority? [Listen]. If it's about seeing more proof, I can keep you updated on results from clients we're onboarding now."

### Technical Objections

**"Will this integrate with our ATS?"**

> "What ATS are you using? [Listen]. We've integrated with [relevant systems]. If we haven't worked with yours specifically, we scope that in the implementation—our team handles all the technical work."

**"What about data security?"**

> "Great question. We don't store your candidate data on our systems—everything stays in your existing tools. We're connecting systems and automating workflows, not replacing your data infrastructure. Happy to go deeper on security if you need specifics for your team."

### Competition Objections

**"We're looking at [Competitor]"**

> "Good to do your research. What appeals to you about them? [Listen]. Here's how I see the difference: [Competitor] is a tool—you buy it, you implement it yourself, you figure out how to make it work. We're a partner—we implement, we train, we optimize. The question is: do you have internal resources to own a tool implementation, or do you need someone to handle it end-to-end?"
